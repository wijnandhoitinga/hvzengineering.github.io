Hoitinga & van Zwieten (HvZ) Computational Engineering is a young consultancy company that specializes in building computer simulations for physical processes, by casting them into mathematical models and subsequently solving these by numerical methods. Well designed computer simulations give valuable understanding of many solid and fluid mechanical problems in engineering.

The ability to model and simulate physical processes makes HvZ a valuable asset in the product design phase of our clients. Instead of optimizing manufactured prototypes, HvZ aims to optimize the virtual prototypes, items that exist only in the computer. This approach can lead to a substantial cost reduction as it will ultimately lead to fewer manufactured prototypes.

In addition, HvZ is able to develop its own dedicated software tools during the virtual design phase, which allows them to operate where most competition cannot follow. HvZ can provide its clients the license-free software tools which, if desired, enable them to play an active role in the virtual prototyping process. An additional advantage of in-house software development is an unmatched flexibility with regard to non-conventional design issues.

We remark that our consultancy services are not restricted to off site work only. If it is more convenient to work on site, for instance to work in a team or with specific software, we can just as well work at our clients.

Modeling

HvZ specializes in the numerical modeling of problems in the physical world, such as structural or fluid flow problems. This is typically a two-step process, which we shall illustrate with an example. Imagine having an interest in temperature loss of fluid flowing through a pipe, and turning to HvZ for a numerical study.

Step 1 is to translate the physical problem to a mathematical description. In the case at hand we will recognize the physical problem to be a convection diffusion problem, for which the mathematical description is known. We will also define the boundary conditions by asking the client specific questions about the setup. The outcome is a closed mathematical system with a unique solution.

Step 2 is to solve the mathematical system numerically, primarily by means of Finite Element modeling (wikipedia). The result will generally be an approximate solution to the mathematical problem at hand. The degree of accuracy is configurable and is a trade-off with computation time. Design parameters such as the diameter of the pipe will be varied as well.

The distinction between the two steps is important, as it allows two potential error-components in the obtained solution. The first component is associated with a possible mismatch between the physics and the mathematical description. For instance, if the scale of the problem is such that molecular effects are significant but this is not accounted for in the mathematical description, one introduces modeling errors. The second component is concerned with the numerical approximation, for example by unjustly sacrificing accuracy for speed. At HvZ we are constantly aware of these hazards, and will make sure that both error contributions are guarded closely.

By combining mathematical and numerical expertise, HvZ can offer a unique, integral approach to problem solving. We believe that a correct understanding of the mathematical basis is vital for numerical modeling, and that this is what sets us aparts from many competitors. HvZ stands for quality, and we are eager to deliver it.

Software

HvZ develops dedicated software tools that can be licenced to the client at request. These are typically specific tools for a given task, though with as many buttons as desired for the design process. For example, the software can be specific for computing the temperature distribution in a pipe, but may allow the thickness and inlet temperature to vary to examine the effect. The set of requirements and desired flexibility of the tool will be discussed at project intake, where it is our expertise to translate a set of engineering demands to software requirements.

HvZ builds on a long experience of developing numerical software. As a result, rather than writing every new program from scratch, we can reuse components that have been developed over the years. This results in rapid development, as typically only the problem-specific layer needs to be implemented. Further, by building on mature components, the software will be stable and fast. Regarding accuracy, note that we take pride in always verifying our results against the mathematics. Furthermore, we note that at HvZ we invest in developing the latest numerical methods for best results.

Although we are comfortable to work with any computer language, our language of choice is Python, for four primary reasons:

  1. The fact that it is free, which results in a significant cost benefit for our clients.
  2. Its focus on readability, which directly benefits both ourselves and our clients.
  3. Its high level nature, which allows for rapid development without low level distractions, but with the possibility for low level optimizations where necessary.
  4. The wealth of modules, both scientific and otherwise, that are being created and maintained by the world wide open source community.

The last group is one that we at HvZ are most obliged to, and we recognize that our work would not be possible without the hard work of others. HvZ believes firmly in the future of open source, and would like to make its contribution by disclosing all non-problem specific source code to benefit the community. As yet, a lot of work still needs to be done to prepare for this step, but a first version is expected in the third quarter of 2011. We are excited about this and look forward to working closely with the community!

Examples

To get an idea on how HvZ can help with your virtual product design, consider the following examples:

  • Engineering applications that contain tribology aspects. Think of lubrication and friction, air bearings, oil bearings, and applications that contain thin-film fluids.
  • Heat-transfer related problems, such as conditioning of system components and dimensioning of heating/cooling systems.
  • Fluid flow related problems.
  • Deformation and stress computations of constructions (solid mechanics).
  • Coupled problems, e.g. heat-transfer and fluid flow or fluid-structure interaction.

The mathematical and numerical background enables HvZ to quickly understand many other types of problems. Do not hesitate to contact HvZ and ask whether the issues regarding your virtual design can be addressed as well. In addition, we emphasize that HvZ is not restricted to off site work only. If preferred, HvZ can help on site and assist in generic design engineering tasks.
