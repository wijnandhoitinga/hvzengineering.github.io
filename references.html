TU Eindhoven


www.tue.nl

HvZ has strong ties with the Multiscale Engineering Fluid Dynamics (MEFD) group of Eindhoven University of Technology, in particular via the collaborative Nutils project. HvZ plays a dominant role in the development of this framework and provides consultancy towards implementation of new scientific concepts. This symbiosis enables HvZ to use the newest, most advanced techniques in the field of computer simulation of physics, and it forms a unique bridge-position between academia and industry.

ASML


www.asml.com

ASML is the world's leading provider of lithography systems for the semiconductor industry, critical to the production of integrated circuits or microchips. HvZ assists in its development by modeling system components in the design phase, in particular aspects related to fluid flow, heat transfer and thermo-mechanics. Because ASML has well developed experimental facilities, they provide a unique platform to compare numerical experiments with measurements.

Innecs BV


www.innecs.nl

In February 2011 Innecs BV contacted HvZ Computational Engineering with the question to assist in virtual prototyping of (parts of) a gas turbine. HvZ analysed the problem and released a stand-alone, license free Octave code, which enables Innecs to run independent simulations of gas turbines. The code is now used by Innecs in their design phase.

Inviscid


www.inviscid.nl

Inviscid is a young consultancy company specialised in product design aided by modeling and simulation techniques. Inviscid excels in simplifying complex physics problems such that they are ready for computer simulation. In addition, Inviscid has excellent expertise in the field of systems engineering. Inviscid and HvZ maintain a close professional relation.
