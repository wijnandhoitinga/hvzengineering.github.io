Here you can find some of our latests clients and projects.


TU/e

HvZ and the Multiscale Engineering Fluid Dynamics Group (MEFD) of Prof.dr.ir. Van Brummelen from Eindhoven University of Technology form a alliance in the development of advanced software to compute complicated model problems. Students and staff members conduct research on new numerical methods and HvZ provide a platform enabling a quick and easy implementation of these ideas. This development of this platform is known as the nutils project The alliance serves a mutual benefit. The MEFD research group has advanced and flexible low level software tools to their disposal including support. HvZ can include all latest developments in the field of physics simulation, thereby serving industry with the most advanced methods and, moreover, closing the gap between research and practical application.

Please visit MEFD and Nutils and for more info.


Innecs BV

In February 2011 Innecs BV contacted HvZ Computational Engineering with the question to assist in virtual prototyping of (parts of) a gas turbine. Within several weeks HvZ analysed and adequately tackled the problem resulting in the release of a stand-alone, license free Octave code, which is now used by Innecs in their design phase. This code enables Innecs to run simulations and study gas turbines without having to rely on third parties.

For the company mission of Innecs and their challenges, please visit Innecs BV.


Inviscid

Inviscid is a young consultancy company specialised in product design by employing modeling and simulation techniques. Inviscid excels in simplifying complex problems, which are then ready to be solved using numerical methods. In addition, Inviscid has excellent expertise in the field of systems engineering. Inviscid and HvZ maintain a close professional relation; see Inviscid for more details.


ASML

ASML is the world's leading provider of lithography systems for the semiconductor industry. ASML designs and assembles complex machines that are critical to the production of integrated circuits or microchips. HvZ assists by modeling system components and thereby improving virtual prototyping aspects in the design phase, in particular, aspects related to fluid flow and heat-transfer. Because ASML has well developed experimental facilities, they provide a unique environment to compare numerical experiments with measurements. For more information see ASML.
